# Landing Copy

## Current direction

The landing page is meant to feel direct and useful, not like a polished marketing site.

Current message:

- AI spend gets messy fast
- this tool helps spot obvious waste
- the recommendation is simple enough to act on

## Hero options

### Option 1

Headline:

`See which AI plans your team actually needs.`

Subheadline:

`Add one tool, check the plan against team size, and get a clear recommendation.`

CTA:

`Start Audit`

### Option 2

Headline:

`Check if your AI stack is bigger than your team needs.`

Subheadline:

`We look at plan size, seat count, and monthly spend. Then we show the easiest place to cut cost.`

CTA:

`Start Audit`

### Option 3

Headline:

`Your AI bill is probably simpler than it looks.`

Subheadline:

`Run a quick audit and see which plan looks too expensive for the way your team actually works.`

CTA:

`Start Audit`

## Tone rules

The landing page copy should feel like:

- a founder explaining the problem clearly
- a technical operator writing for another operator

It should not feel like:

- VC pitch copy
- generic AI SaaS copy
- enterprise sales writing

## Why this copy works for the project

It is specific enough to be believable and short enough to feel product-focused.
