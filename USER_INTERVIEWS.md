# User Interviews

## Interview goal

The goal of these conversations would be to validate:

- whether teams actually feel AI tool spend is messy
- what kind of recommendations they trust
- whether a short audit is enough to get them to ask for help

## Target interview personas

### Founder

Small team founder paying for multiple AI tools across engineering and content work.

### Engineering manager

Owns tooling budget loosely, but does not have a clean view of which seats are still useful.

### Ops or finance generalist

Needs a simple way to spot tool overlap without doing a full internal audit.

## Example interview questions

- How many AI tools is your team paying for right now?
- Do you know which plans are actually being used?
- What usually causes these subscriptions to grow?
- Have you ever downgraded a plan after realizing you did not need it?
- Would you trust a tool that gave basic savings recommendations from plan and seat data alone?
- What would make the output feel credible?
- If a report found real savings, what would you do next?

## Early product signals to look for

- “I know we’re overspending, I just haven’t reviewed it.”
- “We added seats fast and never cleaned them up.”
- “We use one tool heavily and the others only occasionally.”
- “I would share this with my cofounder or finance lead.”

## Product risks interviews should test

- whether users want multi-tool audits instead of one-tool flows
- whether users care more about API spend than seat subscriptions
- whether the report feels specific enough to act on

## Why this matters

The product only works if users believe the recommendations are grounded in real operating logic, not generic AI advice.
