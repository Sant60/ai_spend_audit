# Economics

## Product model

This MVP works best as a lead-generation product rather than a stand-alone high-ARPU SaaS from day one.

The likely business model is:

- free audit tool
- lead capture at the end of the report
- paid advisory, implementation, or finance review after the audit

## Simple unit economics assumption

These are rough planning assumptions, not real company numbers.

### Top-of-funnel

- 100 landing page visitors
- 20 start an audit
- 12 finish an audit
- 3 submit contact info

### Conversion

- 1 out of 3 leads books a follow-up
- 1 out of 1 booked calls converts to a paid service engagement in the best early case

## Cost profile

Main variable costs:

- hosting
- Anthropic summary generation
- engineering time

Given the summary is short and only generated once per audit, AI cost should stay low for early traffic.

## Why this is attractive as a wedge

The app does not need to monetize directly at first if it can:

- surface a real pain point
- produce a screenshot-friendly result
- collect qualified leads with clear spend signals

That makes it useful as an inbound qualifier for Credex.

## Risks

- teams may want richer pricing coverage than this MVP supports
- some teams may expect API usage analysis, not just seat-plan checks
- if the recommendations feel too generic, conversion to follow-up will drop

## What would improve economics

- more accurate pricing coverage
- stronger share loops from result pages
- clearer service handoff after the report
- segmented landing pages for founders, eng managers, and ops leads
